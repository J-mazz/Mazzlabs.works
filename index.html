About Me

I'm currently studying Computer Science at Rogue Community College in a transfer pathway with Oregon State University, with expected completion of my undergrad in Fall 2028. I stand apart from the conventional path though, as I have been working in non-technical jobs for nearly a decade and have recently made the choice to fully commit to pursuing my interests in technology.

My technical journey began with a fascination in machine learning's potential after Google's famous AlphaGo demonstrated its supremacy through ML. I have always been a computer type, having grown up with the technology and developed a preference for the command line at a young age. That naturally led to Linux, shell automation, and eventually Python—which is when I started to realize how much you can really do with the right paradigm. The robust Python libraries allowed for a high level of success in ML even without graduate-level linear algebra comprehension, though high-level mathematics remains a main focus in my formal collegiate pursuit.

I have tinkered with ML projects plenty and have had some success in getting solid learning for both me and the models, but I want more than just training features—I want to produce comprehensive solutions, end-to-end products. Learning TypeScript, JavaScript, HTML, and CSS has been super empowering as a student, and working with Node.js and React to integrate APIs of much more powerful ML solutions than I could reasonably train myself has opened up entirely new possibilities. The GitHub Student Developer Pack has been essentially empowering my educational journey, and my project-based methods have been more than productive—they have been fascinating and compelling.

The Story Behind the Code

Veritas-Lens: Illuminating Media Bias

In an age of information overload, discerning truth from spin becomes crucial. Veritas-Lens leverages BERT transformers to classify political bias across Left, Center, and Right perspectives with 82.15% validation accuracy. This open-source tool democratizes media literacy, helping communities make more informed decisions. github.com/J-mazz/veritas-lens

RipCity Ticket Dispatch: Smart Event Discovery

Finding great events in Portland shouldn't require hunting across multiple platforms. RipCity Ticket Dispatch is a consumer-facing platform that aggregates events from Ticketmaster and Eventbrite, tracks ticket prices, and sends intelligent alerts when deals match user preferences. Built with React and Node.js, this project represents my first attempt at developing an income stream from my programming interests, exploring subscription models and API integrations. ripcityticketdispatch.works

Robust-Cite: Precision in Academic Research

Academic integrity depends on accurate citations, yet parsing bibliographic data remains tedious and error-prone. Robust-Cite solves this with BERT-based named entity recognition, achieving 99.97% validation accuracy across 30,000 citation sequences. By automating what researchers do manually, it frees scholars to focus on discovery rather than formatting. github.com/J-mazz/robust-cite
